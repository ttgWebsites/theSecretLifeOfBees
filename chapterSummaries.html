Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter #1
  • Lily Ray lives with her abusive father, whom she calls T. Ray, and their black housekeeper Rosaleen.
  • Lily is fourteen years old, her mother died when she was four.
  • Lily believes she may have played a major role in the death of her mother. She remembers the day her mother died.
  • Her parents argued as her mother packed her things, the two struggled and her mother dropped a gun.
  • Lily remembers picking up the gun then hearing a loud noise. Lily lives in a small town in South Carolina, where her father is a peach farmer.
  • She sells peaches on the side of the road, which she hates. T. Ray prevents Lily from participating in any school activities or cultivating friendships and she has to sew her own clothes
  • Lily catches a bee in a jar, to the disapproval of Rosaleen.
  • Rosaleen loves Lily and is the closest thing she has to a mother.
  • Lily’s keeps her memories of her mother in a box buried in the orchard; in the box there is a photo, a pair of white gloves and wooden picture of a black Mary with the words Tiburon, S.C. on the back.
  • Whenever Lily feels alone or unhappy, she digs up the box. After a day spent selling peaches on the road, Lily return home to find Rosaleen watching a TV announcement that the Civil Rights Act had been signed.
  • Lily digs up the box that night and falls asleep in the orchard. She is woken by her father who then punishes her by making her kneel on dried grits until her knees are bleeding.
  • Rosaleen and Lily go into town the next day so that Rosaleen can register to vote. It is Lily’s fourteenth birthday.
  • In town, three white men harass Rosaleen. Rosaleen pours the contents of her snuff jar on their feet. The man beat her and have her and Lily taken to jail.
  • Chapter #2-3
  • T. Ray picks lily up from the jail, leaving Rosaleen.
  • He informs Lily that the men will kill Rosaleen. Lily realizes that she needs to run away.
  • She finds Rosaleen at the hospital because she has been beaten even more. She and Rosaleen escape the hospital and head towards Tiburon, the town written on the back of her mother’s picture.
  • Lily awakes the next morning in the forest feeling optimistic. She wonders why her mother had the picture of a black Virgin Mary since they were not Catholic.
  • Lily and Rosaleen walk the rest of the way to Tiburon and Lily goes to the general store to get them something to eat.
  • While at the store, Lily sees honey for sale with the same picture of black Mary that her mother had.
  • She discovers that a woman named August Boatwright sells the honey.
  • Chapter #4-5
  • Lily and Rosaleen travel to the Boatwright’s house. Sisters August, June, and May inhabit the bright pink house.
  • Lily lies and tells the sisters that her father had died and they were traveling to go stay with a relative.
  • August invites them to stay in the honey house. Lily likes it at the Boatwright’s bee farm, despite being the only white person there.
  • August runs the farm with a boy named Zach.
  • May has a breakdown one night and must be brought to her special wall covered with pieces of paper.
  • The sisters introduce Lily to their religion, a mixture of Catholicism and slave-religion.
  • As the news of desegregation in the South is reported, Lily grows self-conscious of her skin color.
  • August teaches Lily about honey farming and explains May’s mental illness as well as the suicide of their other sister, April.
  • Lily hopes to discover some information about her mother from the sisters, but does not want to tell them who she really is.
  • Chapter #6-7
  • Lily meets Neil, June’s boyfriend whom she refuses to marry, and wonders why none of the sisters are married.
  • Lily witnesses a service of the Boatwright’s religion, which focuses on the statue of black Mary.
  • As Lily adjusts to life with the sister’s, she meets Zach, the African American boy who helps at the farm.
  • Zach is a junior in high school and Lily finds herself extremely attracted to him. She tells him that she wants to be a writer and he explains his desire to be a lawyer.
  • They continue to develop romantic feelings for each other.
  • Rosaleen moves from the honey house into the main house, to share a room with May. June and Lily’s relationship continues to be tense and Lily desperately wants to tell August the truth.
  • Zach gives Lily a journal to write her stories in, but tells her that they can never be together because of their races.
  • Chapter #8-9
  • One day Lily and August discuss their lives and the things they love while applying labels to honey jars.
  • August explains bees to Lily and tells her that they need to talk, making Lily uneasy.
  • At lunch Zach tells the women a rumor that a white movie star is coming to town and plans to see a movie with his black girlfriend.
  • After lunch, Lily accompanies Zach to deliver honey to the office of a lawyer, Clayton Forrest.
  • While there, Lily calls T. Ray, he gets angry with Lily and threatens to find her and hurt her.
  • She writes a letter to T. Ray when she gets home, telling him she does not love him and he has been a horrible father to her, she tears the letter up after she is finished.
  • The next day, a bee stings Lily; August says this makes her a real beekeeper.
  • On their way back to the house August and Lily find Rosaleen and May having a water-fight, they join in and soon June does as well.
  • Later Lily asks May if she knew a woman named Deborah Fontanel (Lily’s mother), May says she does.
  • Lily spends the next few days consumed with thoughts of her mother. She decides to speak with August about her mother, but August is busy.
  • Lily goes into town with Zach and they see a group of white men gathered in front of the movie theater waiting for the movie star with the black girlfriend.
  • There is also a group of black teenagers at the theater; one of them throws a bottle at the white men.
  • Police arrest all of the boys, including innocent Zach. Lily must walk home and when she arrives she is told not to tell May about Zach’s arrest.
  • The sisters are worried about how the news would affect May’s fragile mind. Lily goes and visits Zach in jail.
  • May finds out, from Zach’s mother, about his arrest and reacts quite calmly, which is surprising to her sisters.
  • Chapter #10-11
  • May goes out by herself. After a while Lily, August, Rosaleen, and June begin to worry, they cannot find May, and call the police.
  • The police find May’s body in the river. May has committed suicide.
  • The women hold vigil and Zach comes to pay his respects after he is released from jail.
  • During this time Lily begins to feel as though she belongs with the Boatwright’s, despite her race.
  • Things begin to return to normal the week after May’s suicide.
  • The women prepare to celebrate Mary Day, a holiday for the Boatwright’s religion. During this time, Neil arrives and proposes to June.
  • This time, she accepts.
  • That night, the Daughters of Mary along with Neil and Zach, come to the Boatwright house to celebrate the holiday.
  • They all feast and gather to worship the black Mary statue.
  • Lily heads outside, followed by Zach, and they kiss. They promise that they will find a way to be together despite their different races.
  • Chapter #12-13
  • Lily goes to August’s room to ask about her mother.
  • August tells Lily that she worked for her mother’s family as Deborah, Lily’s mother, was growing up.
  • T. Ray began their marriage as a good husband, but her mother grew depressed.
  • She tells her that Deborah ran away to the Boatwright’s, but she returned home to get Lily and that was when she was killed.
  • Lily is very upset to hear that her mother left without her and breaks a bunch of honey jars.
  • The next morning, Rosaleen helps Lily clean up the broken jars and bandage her cuts from the glass.
  • August gives Lily all of the things that she has of Deborah’s, while looking at a picture, Lily realizes that her mother loved her.
  • Chapter #14
  • Lily takes time to mourn for all of the things that she has learned about her mother. When she is ready to move on, Lily finds Rosaleen in a new dress.
  • Rosaleen tells Lily that she is going to register to vote.
  • Zach tells Lily that he will be attending the white high school the next year.
  • While working with the bees, August tells Lily that she must keep her mother alive within herself to rely on when she needs strength.
  • T. Ray shows up at the Boatwright house. He had traced the number Lily had called him from.
  • He yells and pushes Lily and in her frightened state she calls him Daddy, which she has never done.
  • This stops T. Ray’s violence, but he still orders Lily to return home with him.
  • August intervenes and tells T. Ray that she would consider it a favor if she would let Lily live with them.
  • He agrees and as he is driving away, Lily stops him and asks who shot her mother. T. Ray admits that it was Lily who killed her.
  • The following school year, Lily and Zach both attend the local white high school.
  • She becomes a part of the Boatwright family and forgives her mother, because she now has the Boatwright’s and Daughters of Mary as her mothers.
