Characters

  • Lily Owens
  • Lily is a fourteen year old girl, and the narrator of the story, struggling to find her own identity by looking through the past of her deceased mother, she clings onto all the few things she has left of her mother
  • She is tormented by neighborhood boys, and also her father T. Ray
  • She is very creative and desires a better situation in her life, she dreams of attending a manors school, or perhaps being a successful writer
  • T. Ray Owens
  • He is Lily's father, and he owns a peach orchard
  • T. Ray is a very abusive father and a harsh man, Lily doesn't even refer to him as dad, but instead T. Ray, like everyone else
  • Rosaleen Daise
  • Rosaleen is Lily's maid, as well as maid to many of Lily's neighbors
  • She and Lily are very close, and she even plays somewhat of a motherly role for Lily
  • She is a very stubborn and hard-headed woman, yet she always has the best intentions in mind
  • August Boatwright
  • She is the eldest of the Boatwright sisters, and is a very well respected businesswoman in her community
  • She is very wise, and a very deep thinker and she values her spiritual life
  • June Boatwright
  • She is a school teacher and avid musician
  • She was left at the alter early in her adult life and has decided against marriage ever since, the experience also leaves her with a rough outlook on life
  • Uses her musical talents to perform for dying people in hospitals to help soothe their passing
  • May Boatwright
  • A very sensitive and compassionate woman, perhaps oversensitive
  • She had a twin, however the twin died, which could explain her over emotional and sensitive state
  • May cares deeply for others, and spends time at her "wailing wall" when she feels particularly upset about a situation
  • Deborah Fontanel-Owens
  • Lily's dead mother, she is remembered very fondly and was well loved by many
  • Lily accidentally killed her when she was four by picking up a handgun that went off
  • Zach Taylor
  • He is August's godson. Zach is a very hard working and driven young man
  • A very skilled athlete with potential to go to college playing football, however he decides he would rather pursue being a lawyer which would make him the first black lawyer in the area
  • Neil
  • Neil is the principal at June's school, and he harbors a deep love for June
  • He is a very well respected man, and he is adored by June's sisters
  • Neil proposes often to June, yet is always rejected, even though June's sisters hope she will say yes
  • The Daughters of Mary
  • Cressie, Queenie, Violet (Queenie's daughter), Lunelle, Mabelee, and Sugar-Girl, are all followers of Our Lady of Change
  • There is one man in the group, Otis
  • All the women wear elaborate hats made by Lunelle
