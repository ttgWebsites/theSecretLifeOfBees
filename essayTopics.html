Essay Topics

  • Explain Lily's relationship with her dead mother.
  • Lily never had a chance to get to know her mother, and only knows what her father has told her.
  • Initially Lily believed her mother had died when she was very young, and Lily spends time fantasizing about seeing her mother once again.
  • Lily has built her mother up in her mind as a perfect person, and it is something that comforts her while she lives with her abusive father.
  • Lily learns something however that alters her perception of her mother forever.
  • She learns that her mother actually left Lily with T. Ray before she eventually died.
  • Upon learning this Lily is devastated, she learns that her mother, despite how she elevated her memory, was flawed.
  • Lily has a very hard time dealing with this information and resents her mother very much at first.
  • Eventually Lily does manage to accept that her mother was human just like any one else, she accepts her flaws, and comes to forgive her letting go of any resentment she had.
  • How does Lily's coming of age relate to the United States growth and development as a country?
  • As Lily grows up throughout the novel so does the United States.
  • Monk Kidd provides plenty of examples of this as she shows a man walk on the moon, banners for the presidential election as well as the Vietnam war, and even mentions the beginning of the Civil Rights movement.
  • The author makes it clear that the novel is set during a very important time in America, as the country is changing drastically.
  • The changes in America also have a profound effect on Lily's growth into a young woman.
  • Growing up in the south during the time of the Civil Rights movement racial tension was very high.
  • This racial tension leads to events such as Rosaleen getting arrested, Zach's arrest, and May's suicide.
  • These events are influential in Lily's decision to run away with Rosaleen, which will eventually shape the course of Lily's life.
